<h1 style="margin-top: 1rem;">About me</h1>

Hello! I am an undergraduate student at Vanderbilt University studying mathematics and economics, with plans to pursue a PhD in economics. My research interests include health, labor, and education economics, with a focus on inequality and access to opportunity.

I am currently a research assistant at the [Center for Research on Inequality and Health](https://www.vanderbilt.edu/research-inequality-health/) under Dr. Gilbert Gonzales, where I use National Health Interview Survey data to study trends in health outcomes and access to care by sexual orientation, implementing complex survey design and estimating logistic models with adjusted marginal effects for an ongoing manuscript.

Previously, I was a research assistant in the [Research on Conflict and Collective Action Laboratory](https://lab.vanderbilt.edu/rocca/), where I replicated survey-based preference models and constructed cross-national datasets using Stata and Python, including NLP-based classification

I am also an incoming scholar in the [American Economic Association Summer Program](https://www.aeaweb.org/about-aea/committees/aeasp).
