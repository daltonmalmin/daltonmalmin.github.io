<h1 style="margin-top: 1rem;">About me</h1>

Hello! I am an undergraduate student at Vanderbilt University studying mathematics and economics, with plans to pursue a PhD in economics. My research interests include health, labor, and education economics, with a focus on inequality and access to opportunity.

I am currently a research assistant at the [Center for Research on Inequality and Health](https://www.vanderbilt.edu/research-inequality-health/) under Dr. Gilbert Gonzales, where I work with National Health Interview Survey data to study trends in health outcomes and access to care by sexual orientation. My work involves data construction, survey-weighted analysis, and contributing to an ongoing manuscript examining national trends from 2013 to 2024.

Previously, I was a research assistant in the [Research on Conflict and Collective Action Laboratory](https://lab.vanderbilt.edu/rocca/), where I worked with Dr. Brett V. Benson and Dr. J. Andrés Gannon on projects related to international relations and military capabilities, including replication of survey-based preference models and development of cross-national datasets using Python.

I am also an incoming scholar in the [American Economic Association Summer Program](https://www.aeaweb.org/about-aea/committees/aeasp).
